By the N4 level, memorizing characters by rote becomes inefficient. The key is to study **Radicals (部首)**.

### The Anatomy of a Kanji
Most N4 Kanji are composed of a semantic part (meaning) and a phonetic part (reading). Identifying the "building blocks" helps in guessing the meaning of new characters before checking a dictionary.

### The N4 Goal
My current strategy is focusing on the remaining characters needed for the JLPT N4. I prioritize learning them in context with vocabulary rather than in isolation. For example, instead of just learning **新** (new), I learn it as part of **新聞** (newspaper - *shinbun*), which also reinforces the Kanji for "hear/listen".



I recommend using a spaced repetition system (SRS) like [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) to keep these 300+ characters in your long-term memory while you bridge the gap to N3.
